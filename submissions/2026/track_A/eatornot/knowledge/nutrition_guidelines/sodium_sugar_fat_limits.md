# Sodium, Sugar, and Fat Limits

## Sodium

### Daily Limits
- Recommended: <2000mg/day
- Maximum: <3000mg/day
- Danger: >5000mg/day

### Per Meal Guidelines
- Ideal: <600mg
- Acceptable: <800mg
- High: >1000mg

### Health Risks
- High blood pressure
- Heart disease
- Stroke
- Kidney problems

### Fast Food Reality
- Most fast food meals: 800-1500mg sodium
- Single burger: 500-1000mg
- Fries (medium): 200-300mg

## Sugar

### Daily Limits
- Added sugar: <50g/day (WHO)
- Ideal: <25g/day
- Sugary drink: 30-50g per serving

### Health Risks
- Weight gain
- Type 2 diabetes
- Tooth decay
- Inflammation

### Common Sources
- Sugary drinks (cola, juice)
- Desserts
- Sauces (ketchup, BBQ)
- Breakfast items

## Fat

### Daily Limits
- Total fat: 20-35% of calories
- Saturated fat: <10% of calories
- Trans fat: <1% of calories

### Per Meal Guidelines
- Total fat: <25g
- Saturated fat: <10g

### Types
- Unsaturated (healthy): nuts, fish, avocado
- Saturated (limit): meat, dairy, coconut oil
- Trans (avoid): fried foods, processed snacks

- WHO Guidelines
