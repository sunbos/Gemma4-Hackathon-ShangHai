# BMI and TDEE Notes

## BMI (Body Mass Index)

### Formula
BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)²

### Categories
- Underweight: <18.5
- Normal: 18.5-24.9
- Overweight: 25-29.9
- Obese: ≥30

### Limitations
- Doesn't account for muscle mass
- Athletes may be "overweight" by BMI
- Distribution of fat not considered
- Age and ethnicity factors ignored

## BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)

### What It Is
- Calories burned at complete rest
- Energy for basic life functions
- 60-75% of total daily expenditure

### Mifflin-St Jeor (Preferred)
- Men: 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age + 5
- Women: 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age - 161

### Factors Affecting BMR
- Muscle mass (more = higher BMR)
- Age (decreases with age)
- Genetics
- Hormones

## TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

### Components
- BMR: 60-75%
- Activity: 15-30%
- Thermic effect of food: 10%

### Activity Multipliers
- Sedentary: BMR × 1.2
- Light: BMR × 1.375
- Moderate: BMR × 1.55
- Active: BMR × 1.725
- Very Active: BMR × 1.9

### For Weight Loss
- Eat below TDEE
- Target TDEE - 300 to -500
- Recalculate as weight changes

## Practical Application
1. Calculate BMI for category
2. Calculate BMR for baseline
3. Apply activity multiplier for TDEE
4. Subtract deficit for daily target
5. Divide by meals for meal budget
