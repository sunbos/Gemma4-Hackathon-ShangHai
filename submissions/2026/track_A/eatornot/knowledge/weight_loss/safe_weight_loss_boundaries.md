# Safe Weight Loss Boundaries

## Safe Rates

### General Rule
- 0.5-1 kg per week maximum
- ~1% of body weight per week
- Slower is more sustainable

### By BMI
- Normal BMI: 0.25-0.5 kg/week
- Overweight: 0.5-1 kg/week
- Obese: up to 1 kg/week initially

## Warning Signs

### Too Fast
- Losing >1 kg/week consistently
- Extreme fatigue
- Hair loss
- Irregular periods (women)
- Muscle cramps

### Restrictive Eating
- Eating below 1200 kcal (women) or 1500 kcal (men)
- Eliminating entire food groups
- Obsessive calorie counting
- Anxiety around food

## When to Stop/Slow Down

### Medical Conditions
- Pregnant or breastfeeding
- Eating disorder history
- Chronic illness
- Under 18 years old

### Signs to Watch
- Dizziness or fainting
- Heart palpitations
- Severe mood changes
- Loss of menstrual cycle

## Professional Help

### When to Consult Doctor
- BMI <18.5 and trying to lose weight
- Rapid unexplained weight loss
- Co-existing medical conditions
- Taking medications affecting weight

### When to See Dietitian
- Need personalized meal planning
- Food allergies/intolerances
- Complex medical conditions
- Long-term weight management

## Sustainability

### Long-term Success
- Focus on habits, not just weight
- Include foods you enjoy
- Allow flexibility
- Build sustainable lifestyle

### Maintenance Phase
- Gradually increase calories
- Continue healthy habits
- Monitor weight weekly
- Adjust as needed
