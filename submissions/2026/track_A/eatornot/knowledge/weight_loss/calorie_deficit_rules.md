# Calorie Deficit Rules

## Basic Principle
- Weight loss requires calorie deficit
- Calories in < Calories out = weight loss
- 1 kg body fat ≈ 7700 kcal

## Deficit Sizes

### Moderate (Recommended)
- 300-500 kcal/day deficit
- ~0.3-0.5 kg loss per week
- Sustainable long-term
- Minimal muscle loss

### Aggressive
- 500-750 kcal/day deficit
- ~0.5-0.7 kg loss per week
- Harder to sustain
- Some muscle loss risk

### Extreme (Not Recommended)
- >1000 kcal/day deficit
- Rapid loss but unsustainable
- Significant muscle loss
- Health risks

## Creating Deficit

### Through Diet
- Reduce portion sizes
- Choose lower calorie foods
- Limit sugary drinks
- Reduce processed foods

### Through Exercise
- Walking: ~300 kcal/hour
- Running: ~600 kcal/hour
- Swimming: ~500 kcal/hour
- Strength training: preserves muscle

### Combined (Best)
- 250 kcal from diet
- 250 kcal from exercise
- = 500 kcal total deficit

## Minimum Calories
- Women: never below 1200 kcal/day
- Men: never below 1500 kcal/day
- Below these: nutrient deficiency risk

## Plateau Management
- Body adapts to lower calories
- Recalculate TDEE as weight changes
- Adjust deficit accordingly
- Include diet breaks (1-2 weeks at maintenance)
