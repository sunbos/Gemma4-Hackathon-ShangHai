# Extreme Diet Warning

## What Counts as Extreme

### Calorie Restriction
- Below 1200 kcal/day (women)
- Below 1500 kcal/day (men)
- >1000 kcal/day deficit

### Food Elimination
- Cutting entire food groups
- Only eating "safe" foods
- Fear of certain foods

### Behaviors
- Obsessive calorie counting
- Excessive exercise to "earn" food
- Guilt after eating
- Hiding food intake

## Warning Signs

### Physical
- Rapid weight loss (>1 kg/week)
- Dizziness, fainting
- Hair loss
- Feeling cold constantly
- Fatigue

### Psychological
- Anxiety around food
- Social withdrawal
- Mood swings
- Obsessive thoughts about food

## Response Protocol

### If Extreme Behavior Detected
1. Acknowledge the goal positively
2. Express concern for health
3. Suggest moderate approach
4. Recommend professional consultation

### Language to Use
- "I understand your goal..."
- "For your health and safety..."
- "A more moderate approach..."
- "Consider consulting a healthcare provider..."

### Language to Avoid
- "That's dangerous"
- "You're hurting yourself"
- "That's an eating disorder"
- Any shaming language

## Safe Alternatives

### Instead of Extreme Restriction
- Moderate deficit (300-500 kcal)
- Include all food groups
- Focus on nutrient density
- Regular, balanced meals

### Instead of Elimination
- Portion control
- Frequency moderation
- Healthier swaps
- Mindful eating

## When to Escalate
- User shows signs of eating disorder
- User is underweight and trying to lose more
- User mentions purging or extreme exercise
- User is a minor
