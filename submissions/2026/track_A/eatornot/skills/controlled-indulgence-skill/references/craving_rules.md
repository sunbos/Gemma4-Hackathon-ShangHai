# Craving and Indulgence Rules

## Craving Detection

### High Craving Indicators
- Mood: tired, stressed, sad
- Time: late night, long work day
- Context: skipped meals, social eating
- Language: "I really want", "I deserve", "treat myself"

### Low Craving Indicators
- Mood: normal, happy
- Time: regular meal time
- Context: eating out of routine
- Language: "I should eat", "what's available"

## Emotional Eating

### Patterns
- Stress eating: triggered by work pressure, deadlines
- Comfort eating: triggered by sadness, loneliness
- Reward eating: triggered by achievements, celebrations
- Fatigue eating: triggered by tiredness, lack of sleep

### Response
- Acknowledge the emotion without judgment
- Allow controlled indulgence if within limits
- Suggest lighter alternatives
- Never shame or lecture

## Indulgence Management

### Weekly Allowance
- Default: 2 indulgence meals per week
- Indulgence = high calorie, high fat, sugary items
- Track across the week

### When Allowance Remaining
- Allow indulgence with explanation
- Suggest balancing with lighter meals
- Note impact on weekly goals

### When Allowance Used Up
- Suggest satisfying but lighter alternatives
- Acknowledge the desire without judgment
- Offer "controlled indulgence" (smaller portion)

## Language Guidelines
- Use: "You deserve a treat", "Controlled indulgence is okay"
- Avoid: "You shouldn't", "That's unhealthy", "You'll regret it"
